Know the warning signs

Is That a Raccoon in Your Attic?

üëÇ

Heavy Footsteps at Night

Raccoons have heavier footsteps — it almost sounds like a person walking up above you. You'll hear them more in the evening and throughout the night. That's the easiest way to tell them apart from squirrels, which are more of a pitter-patter and scurrying.

üèöÔ∏è

Torn Soffits & Roof Damage

Raccoons love dormers — where two different roof lines meet. They also tear into soffit areas right underneath the gutter. A lot of those are vinyl, and raccoons can puncture through or push through pretty easily. They just sort of tear in — there's not really a rhyme or reason.

üêæ

Paw Prints & Staining Around Entry Points

Raccoons are pretty dirty, so you'll see tracks leading up to entry points. They also have natural oils on their fur, so when they're going in and out, it makes natural discoloration around the opening. That helps us pinpoint where they're getting in.

üîç

Smaller Entry Points Than You'd Think

That's a lot of fur. They look pretty large, but they can squeeze into smaller points of entry than you'd think. And if they can get leverage — even on an aluminum downspout — they'll scoot up almost anything.

üì∑
From our technicians: When we get new calls, we go down the line — when do you hear the noises? What do they sound like? Heavy footsteps and chittering at night is almost always raccoons. Pitter-pattering and chewing during the day is usually squirrels. Most of the time, homeowners don't really know what they're dealing with. We ask specialized filter questions to give our technicians a preemptive idea of what they're going to need to be looking for.

Humane Exclusion

We install what's called a check valve — an exclusion door. It looks like a trap, but it doesn't have an end to it. It just has a flap that lifts up so the raccoon can go out for food and water, which they're naturally going to do, and then it flips back down when they leave. They can get out, but they can't get back in.

Raccoon Droppings Are a Health Hazard

Raccoon feces contain Baylisascaris (raccoon roundworm) — a serious parasitic hazard that can affect humans and pets. A lot of people call us because they just aren't comfortable going in their attic, and they shouldn't be — this isn't a DIY cleanup job. Our team uses professional-grade PPE, HEPA filtration, and commercial disinfectants.

üè†

A Month vs. Two Years — Very Different Jobs

If they've been in there less than a month, we can probably do a spot cleaning — certain little sections. But if they've been in there for two years and nobody's done anything about it, all those pheromones get mixed into the insulation. Droppings are sporadic all throughout the attic. That usually means full attic insulation removal and replacement.

Full remediations can be covered under homeowner's insurance

What Affects Your Cost

üè†

Type of Home & Roof

A one-story ranch is completely different from a three-story downtown Indy home with Victorian molding or a slate roof. Height, access, and roofing materials all factor in.

üî©

Siding & Materials

Vinyl siding cracks from sun exposure — easy for raccoons to tear into. Aluminum is harder to puncture. Stucco is foam-based. The material your home is made of, top to bottom, makes a difference.

üö™

Number of Entry Points

Some homes have one entry point and everything else is buttoned up tight. Others need a lot of work — sometimes even out of the scope of what we'd normally do. More entry points means more materials and labor.

‚è≥

How Long They've Been There

A raccoon that's been there a few weeks is a simpler job. One that's been there for months means more damage, more cleanup, and a bigger project. The sooner you call, the less it costs.
